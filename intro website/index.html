Challenges & Context

Low kitchen usage, high social isolation

International students living in dorms want nutritious food and cultural exchange, yet opaque information, clunky registration, and limited facilitation keep them from using shared kitchens.

Pain Points

  • 60% of students rarely or never use the communal kitchen.
  • Over half report making few or no new friends in their residence.
  • Complex sign-up flows and messy kitchen conditions suppress motivation.

User Expectations

  • Real-time visibility into open spots and kitchen status.
  • Small-group, structured co-cooking experiences.
  • Tangible rewards or keepsakes to encourage repeat participation.

Our Approach

NutriMate: a hybrid physical-digital co-cooking platform

We connect dormitory common spaces with lightweight digital touchpoints to create a seamless journey from “see an event” to “cook together” to “celebrate the outcome.”

Interactive Lobby Board

Large-format displays in lobbies and elevators show schedules, cultural themes, and kitchen guidelines. Scan a QR code to reserve a slot instantly.

Lightweight Mobile Companion

A mini-app experience lets students check availability, save recipes, and find teammates during small moments on the go.

AI-assisted Kitchen Screen

In-kitchen displays provide nutrition tips, step reminders, and role-based progress visualisation to keep teams coordinated and engaged.

Kitchen Display

Personalised illustrations that reinforce belonging after each session

Social & Mobile Edge

Join anywhere, connect with anyone

NutriMate is designed for students who are always on the move. We bridge physical spaces and online touchpoints to create a low-barrier social network around food.

Live Availability Alerts

Mobile notifications nudge students to act in spare moments so popular slots fill with engaged participants, not just early birds.

Find Your Cooking Partner

Students can match with peers who share language, dietary needs, or grocery plans and agree on roles before the session starts.

Facilitated On-site Interaction

Kitchen displays support synchronous and asynchronous guidance so late arrivals or newcomers can join smoothly.

Impact: higher kitchen utilisation, stronger cross-cultural friendships, and healthier daily routines.

About Team LateBu

We are Team LateBu, a multidisciplinary group in DECO3500 exploring how hybrid social experiences can support healthier everyday lives. Our studio project, NutriMate, focuses on transforming dormitory kitchens into spaces that foster cross-cultural connection and well-being. Each member contributes unique expertise across research, design, and evaluation to bring this vision to life.

Who we are

  • Yinhan Liu · UX Researcher & Interface Designer — conducted user research and interviews, synthesized key insights, and led the design of the Reservation Interface in the prototype; also participated in prototype evaluation and user testing.
  • Yanjie Bu · Service Designer & Visual Designer — carried out user research and interviews, investigated healthy food criteria to support the project concept, designed the Cooking Process and illustration pages, and contributed to prototype testing.
  • Yuzhe Wang · Research Analyst & Evaluation Designer — explored healthy food frameworks and related academic sources, conducted user interviews, designed the prototype evaluation plan and SUS chart, and analyzed testing data to refine the design.
  • Hanqi Liu · Interaction Designer & Project Documenter — facilitated user interviews and insight analysis, contributed to ideation and concept development, maintained the project wiki logs, and will continue to support prototype testing and iteration.
  • Shizhuo Sun · UX Researcher & Prototype Developer — organized and analyzed interview data to identify user pain points, co-created the low-fidelity homepage prototype, and led prototype testing and data analysis to guide future design improvements.

What we focus on

  • Research & Insights: extensive user interviews, healthy eating research, and iterative evaluation to uncover needs and guide design directions.
  • Product & Interaction: development of reservation and cooking interfaces, low-fidelity prototyping, and structured usability testing for hybrid experiences.
  • Visual & Narrative: cohesive storytelling through illustrations, interface design, and documentation to communicate NutriMate’s community-centered vision.
