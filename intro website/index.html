Challenges & Context

Low kitchen usage, high social isolation

International students want nutritious food and cultural exchange, yet opaque information, clunky registration, and limited facilitation discourage them from using shared kitchens.

Pain Points

  • 60% of students rarely or never use the communal kitchen.
  • Over half report making few or no new friends in their residence.
  • Complex sign-up flows and messy kitchen conditions suppress motivation.

User Expectations

  • Real-time visibility into open spots and kitchen status.
  • Small-group, structured co-cooking experiences.
  • Tangible rewards or keepsakes to encourage repeat participation.

Our Approach

NutriMate: a hybrid physical-digital co-cooking platform

We connect dormitory common spaces with lightweight digital touchpoints to create a seamless journey from seeing an event, to cooking together, to celebrating success.

Interactive Lobby Board

Large displays in lobbies and elevators surface weekly events and health notes. Students drag an event tag to reserve a slot and print a ticket instantly.

Lightweight Mobile Companion

A mini-app experience lets students check availability, bookmark recipes, and find teammates while on the move.

AI-assisted Kitchen Screen

In-kitchen displays provide nutrition tips, step reminders, and role-based progress visuals so teams stay aligned and engaged.

Lobby Screen

Weekly timetable, drag-to-reserve interaction, and ticket printing.

Kitchen Display

Step tracking, puzzle-style progress, and contextual health prompts.

Souvenir Printer

Personalised keepsakes reinforce the experience after each session.

Social & Mobile Edge

Shared activity, context-aware support

NutriMate brings people together through shared activity and context-aware tools. It is social because students cook, learn, and create side by side; it is mobile because the experience travels with them from lobby screens, to kitchen surfaces, to their phones.

Cook, Learn, Create Together

Roles, shared tasks, and playful prompts keep every participant engaged so collaboration feels natural, even for first-time partners.

Context-aware Guidance

Lobby timetables, kitchen mission screens, and AI nudges adapt to where students are standing, providing just-in-time cues without extra planning.

Companion on the Move

The mobile view mirrors schedules, nudges sign-ups, and stores keepsakes so the experience continues after students leave the kitchen.

Impact: social connection powered by shared cooking, plus mobile touchpoints that keep momentum wherever students go.

About Team LateBu

We are Team LateBu, a multidisciplinary group in DECO3500 exploring how hybrid social experiences can support healthier everyday lives. Our studio project reimagines dormitory kitchens as hubs for cross-cultural connection and well-being.

Who we are

  • Yinhan Liu - UX Researcher and Interface Designer: leads discovery interviews, synthesises insights, and shapes the reservation interface in our prototypes.
  • Yanjie Bu - Service and Visual Designer: maps the end-to-end journey, defines healthy food criteria, and crafts the visual language for cooking missions.
  • Yuzhe Wang - Research Analyst and Evaluation Designer: plans evaluation cycles, produces the SUS analysis, and translates findings into iteration priorities.
  • Hanqi Liu - Interaction Designer and Project Steward: orchestrates co-design workshops, documents key decisions, and keeps the concept cohesive across touchpoints.
  • Shizhuo Sun - Prototype Developer and UX Researcher: builds interactive demos, runs usability testing, and drives continuous improvements.

What we focus on

  • Research and Insights: in-depth interviews, healthy eating research, and iterative evaluation to ground every decision.
  • Product and Interaction: structured prototyping, reservation and cooking interfaces, and usability testing for hybrid experiences.
  • Visual and Narrative: cohesive storytelling through illustrations, interface design, and documentation that communicate NutriMate values.
