Challenges & Context

Low kitchen usage, high social isolation

International students living in dorms want nutritious food and cultural exchange, yet opaque information, clunky registration, and limited facilitation keep them from using shared kitchens.

Pain Points

  • 60% of students rarely or never use the communal kitchen.
  • Over half report making few or no new friends in their residence.
  • Complex sign-up flows and messy kitchen conditions suppress motivation.

User Expectations

  • Real-time visibility into open spots and kitchen status.
  • Small-group, structured co-cooking experiences.
  • Tangible rewards or keepsakes to encourage repeat participation.

Our Approach

NutriMate: a hybrid physical-digital co-cooking platform

We connect dormitory common spaces with lightweight digital touchpoints to create a seamless journey from “see an event” to “cook together” to “celebrate the outcome.”

Interactive Lobby Board

Large-format displays in lobbies and elevators show schedules, cultural themes, and kitchen guidelines. Scan a QR code to reserve a slot instantly.

Lightweight Mobile Companion

A mini-app experience lets students check availability, save recipes, and find teammates during small moments on the go.

AI-assisted Kitchen Screen

In-kitchen displays provide nutrition tips, step reminders, and role-based progress visualisation to keep teams coordinated and engaged.

Lobby Screen

Kitchen Display

Step tracking · Puzzle progress · AI health prompts

Souvenir Printer

Personalised illustrations that reinforce belonging after each session

Social & Mobile Edge

Join anywhere, connect with anyone

NutriMate is designed for students who are always on the move. We bridge physical spaces and online touchpoints to create a low-barrier social network around food.

Live Availability Alerts

Mobile notifications nudge students to act in spare moments so popular slots fill with engaged participants, not just early birds.

Find Your Cooking Partner

Students can match with peers who share language, dietary needs, or grocery plans and agree on roles before the session starts.

Facilitated On-site Interaction

Kitchen displays support synchronous and asynchronous guidance so late arrivals or newcomers can join smoothly.

Impact: higher kitchen utilisation, stronger cross-cultural friendships, and healthier daily routines.
